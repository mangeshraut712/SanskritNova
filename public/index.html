About Sanskrit

Context matters when you study a language with long literary and scholarly use.

An ancient Indo-Aryan language with continuing scholarly use

Sanskrit is an ancient Indo-Aryan language of the Indo-European family. It is central to Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain textual traditions, and it remains important in ritual, philosophy, literature, scholarship, and cultural study.

Historical arc

Vedic roots, later refinement

Early Vedic material is associated with the Rigveda around 1500 to 1200 BCE. Later, Pāṇini’s grammatical framework became a reference point for Classical Sanskrit.

Why learners care

Texts, thought, precision

Sanskrit opens direct access to the Vedas, the Mahābhārata, the Rāmāyaṇa, the Yogasūtra, and major traditions in grammar, medicine, astronomy, and philosophical reasoning.

1500 BCE

Vedic layer

Earliest attested material appears in the Vedic corpus, especially the Rigveda.

4th c BCE

Pāṇinian refinement

Grammatical codification shapes the polished literary language associated with Classical Sanskrit.

1st millennium

Scholarly lingua franca

Sanskrit circulates widely across literature, ritual, philosophy, and technical writing.

Today

Living heritage

It remains studied globally and used in liturgical, educational, and cultural contexts.
