Vedic Corpus

Complete collection of Vedic texts with critical editions and manuscript provenance

Ṛgveda Saṁhitā

ऋग्वेद संहिता

The oldest of the four Vedas, containing 1,028 hymns organized into 10 mandalas. Critical edition from Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute (1933-1972).

Veda 1500-1200 BCE

Sāmaveda Saṁhitā

सामवेद संहिता

The Veda of melodies and chants, consisting of 1,875 verses primarily drawn from the Rigveda but arranged for liturgical purposes.

Veda 1200-1000 BCE

Yajurveda Saṁhitā

यजुर्वेद संहिता

The Veda of ritual formulas, existing in two main recensions: Shukla Yajurveda (White) and Krishna Yajurveda (Black).

Veda 1200-1000 BCE

Atharvaveda Saṁhitā

अथर्ववेद संहिता

The Veda of magical formulas and daily rituals, containing 731 hymns covering practical aspects of daily life and healing.

Veda 1000-800 BCE

Philosophical Systems

Six orthodox schools of Indian philosophy with complete texts and commentaries

Sāṃkhya

सांख्य

One of the six orthodox philosophical systems, founded by Kapila, emphasizing dualism between consciousness (purusha) and matter (prakriti).

Darshana Classical

Yoga

योग

The philosophical system based on Patanjali's Yoga Sutras, emphasizing the practice of meditation and spiritual discipline for liberation.

Darshana Classical

Vedānta

वेदान्त

The philosophical system based on the Upanishads and Brahma Sutras, encompassing Advaita, Vishishtadvaita, and Dvaita sub-schools.

Darshana Classical

Mīmāṃsā

मीमांसा

The philosophical system focused on interpretation of Vedic rituals, founded by Jaimini with emphasis on dharma and ritual performance.

Darshana Classical

Manuscript Repositories

Extensive collection of 2,000+ Sanskrit manuscripts, particularly strong in Nepalese and Kashmiri palm leaf manuscripts.

Sample Texts

Examples of Sanskrit texts with proper IAST transliteration and translation

ॐ पूर्णमदः पूर्णमिदं पूर्णात् पूर्णमुदच्यते
oṁ pūrṇamadaḥ pūrṇamidaṁ pūrṇāt pūrṇamudacyate
"That is whole, this is whole; from the whole, the whole becomes whole. When the whole is taken away from the whole, the whole still remains." - Isha Upanishad, Verse 1
असतो मा सद्गमय तमसो मा ज्योतिर्गमय
asto mā sadgamaya tamaso mā jyotirgamaya
"Lead me from the unreal to the real, from darkness to light, from death to immortality." - Brihadaranyaka Upanishad, 1.3.28
धर्मो रक्षति रक्षितः
dharmaḥ rakṣati rakṣitaḥ
"Dharma protects those who protect dharma." - Mahabharata, Vana Parva
